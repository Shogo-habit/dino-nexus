# Dinosaur Illustration Style Guide

This document defines the visual style for all dinosaur illustrations in the Dino-Nexus project to ensure consistency across the archive.

## Core Visual Style

- **Technique**: Scientific illustration, naturalist book style.
- **Medium**: High-quality watercolor and detailed ink wash.
- **Line Work**: Fine, detailed ink outlines.
- **Texturing**: Realistic skin textures, scales, or feathers as appropriate for the species, with subtle shading.
- **Aesthetic**: Vintage naturalist encyclopedia feel (19th or early 20th-century style).

## Background & Environment

- **Setting**: Authentic prehistoric environments (Mesozoic era).
- **Elements**: Lush vegetation (ferns, cycads, ginkgo trees, primitive conifers), rocky outcrops, ancient riverbanks, or open floodplains.
- **Lighting**: Natural daylight, soft atmospheric perspective.
- **Atmosphere**: Realistic, wild, and ancient.

## Constraints & Requirements

- **NO Futuristic Elements**: Absolutely no HUDs, digital overlays, glowing parts, metal, or technology.
- **NO Modern Elements**: No modern buildings, clothes, or contemporary debris.
- **Aspect Ratio**: 1:1 (Square).
- **Composition**: Full bleed (no white borders or margins), background must extend to all edges of the image. Full body of the dinosaur, clearly visible and well-framed.
- **Text**: Do NOT include text or labels unless explicitly requested.

## Example Prompt Component

> "A professional scientific illustration of a [Dinosaur Name] in its natural habitat. High-quality watercolor and ink wash style, detailed textures, realistic prehistoric background with ferns and ancient trees. Vintage naturalist book aesthetic. No futuristic elements, no text, square composition."
